Whenever we process your data for one of these purposes, we have determined that one or more of the following lawful bases apply:

 

—Performance of a contract;

3. User’s rights

 

To the extent required by the law of your jurisdiction, you may have below rights. Before you exercise below rights, you may consult your legal counsel if the laws of your jurisdiction stipulate below rights.

3.1 The right to be informed

The User has the right to be provided with clear, transparent and easily understandable information about how V Recorder collects and uses personal data and its rights. This is the reason why V Recorder is providing the User with the information in this privacy policy.

 

3.2 The right of access

The User has the right to obtain access to its personal data (if V Recorder is processing it), and other certain information (similar to that provided in this privacy policy).

 

3.3 The right to rectification

The User is entitled to have its personal data corrected if it’s inaccurate or incomplete.

 

3.4 The right to erasure

This ‘the right to be forgotten’ enables the User to request the deletion or removal of its personal data where there’s no compelling reason for V Recorder to keep using it. This is not a general right to erasure; there are exceptions.

 

3.5  The right to restrict processing

Under certain circumstances, the User has the rights to ‘block’ or suppress further use of its personal data.

 

3.6 The right to data portability

The User has the rights to receive its personal data provided to V Recorder in a structured, commonly used and machine readable format and has the right to transmit those data to another controller.

 

3.7 The right to object to processing

The User has the right to object, on grounds relating to its particular situation, at any time, to the processing of its personal data.

 

3.8 The right to lodge a complaint

The User has the right to lodge a complaint about the way V Recorder handles or processes its personal data with its national supervisory authority (in France, the CNIL).

 

3.9 The right to withdraw consent

If the user has given its consent for a specific processing of its personal data implemented by V Recorder, the User has the right to withdraw its consent at any time. In case the User does so, it does not mean that anything V Recorder has done with the User’s personal data with its consent up to that point is unlawful).

 

3.10 The right to define instructions

The User has the right to define general or specific instructions regarding storage, deletion and use of its personal data after death.
